Early Life and Career Beginnings

Mick Mars, originally named Robert Alan Deal, was born in Terre Haute, Indiana, in 1951. He moved to Huntington, Indiana, in his early years before settling in Garden Grove, California, with his family.

Mars dropped out of high school and played guitar in several unsuccessful blues rock bands in the 1970s. However, his passion for music and determination to succeed never wavered.

In 1980, Mars decided to take a bold step towards his musical career. He put out an ad in the Los Angeles newspaper The Recycler, describing himself as a “loud, rude, and aggressive guitar player.” This caught the attention of Tommy Lee and Nikki Sixx, who were in the process of forming a new band.

Impressed by Mars’ skills and attitude, Lee approached him to join the band they were forming, which would later become the legendary Mötley Crüe. Mars gladly accepted the offer and became their lead guitarist, playing a pivotal role in the band’s success.

With Mars’ exceptional guitar playing and songwriting abilities, Mötley Crüe quickly gained recognition within the rock music industry. The band’s formation marked the beginning of a journey that would solidify their place as one of the most iconic and influential rock bands of all time.

Mick Mars’ early life and career beginnings laid the foundation for his extraordinary musical journey with Mötley Crüe. His unwavering dedication and talent would go on to shape not only his own success but also the legacy of the band itself.
